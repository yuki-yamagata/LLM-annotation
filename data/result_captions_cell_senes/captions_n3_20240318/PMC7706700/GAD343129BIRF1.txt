Organization and output of results:
The image illustrates the central role of senescent cells in various biological processes and outcomes. Senescent cells can promote immune cell recruitment, reinforce their own senescence, influence cell fate and differentiation, promote tumor growth, contribute to tissue dysfunction and inflammation, evade the immune system, and induce senescence in neighboring cells through paracrine effects. They can also influence the plasticity and stemness of cells. Conversely, the presence of senescent cells can negatively impact the processes of wound healing and fibrosis resolution. This complex network of interactions highlights the multifaceted impact of cellular senescence on organismal health and disease.
